## Lighting

Good lighting is the secret weapon that makes budget cameras look expensive. A $50 webcam with great lighting beats a $500 camera in bad lighting every time.

### Budget

{{product:ring-light}}

The ring light is streaming lighting 101. Affordable, easy to set up, and immediately improves your image quality.

The circular design creates a distinctive catchlight in your eyes (that ring reflection) which makes you look more engaged and alive. The soft, even light is naturally flattering.

Setup tips: Position at eye level, slightly in front of and above your monitor. Use the daylight (5500K) color temperature setting. Dim it down if it's too harsh, more light isn't always better.

Every single beginner streamer should get one today. This is the highest value upgrade you can make.

### Mid-Range

{{product:elgato-key-light}}

Elgato's Key Light is what happens when a gaming company makes professional lighting. It's smart, adjustable, and designed specifically for streamers.

Change brightness and color temperature from your phone or Stream Deck without leaving your chair. Save presets for day vs. night streaming. Sync multiple lights for consistent lighting.

The Key Light produces that clean, professional look you see in high-production streams. The edge-lit design eliminates harsh shadows while providing powerful, consistent illumination.

For streamers ready to invest in production quality. If streaming is bringing in income or you're serious about growth, proper lighting is more impactful than upgrading your camera.

Many streamers use two Key Lights, one as key light (main light) and one as fill light (softer, angled from the side) for a professional three-point lighting setup.

### DIY Option

{{product:softbox-lights}}

If you want professional lighting on a budget, photography soft boxes deliver incredible results for the price.

The diffused light is incredibly flattering and professional-looking. You can position them for proper three-point lighting (key, fill, and hair light) without spending $600 on three Elgato lights.

The trade-offs: larger footprint than ring lights or Key Lights, no app control or presets, manual adjustment only, not gaming-branded (if that matters to you).

Budget-conscious streamers who want professional results, anyone with space for light stands, or streamers who also do photography and want multipurpose equipment.
